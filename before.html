Before

Application Process

Back in 2018, I was a 22 year-old student attending Grand Rapids Community College (GRCC) in my hometown of Grand Rapids, Michigan. I was also a member and Peer Mentor for GRCC TRIO Student Support Services (SSS) Program, a federally funded program that aimed to assist first generation low-income college students. Over the previous year, my advisors kept encouraging me to apply to the Council of Opportunity in Education (COE) Keith Sherin Global Leaders Program, a summer-semester study abroad program open for TRIO students nation-wide. Being the reserved, cautious and timid person I was, I didn’t know if I wanted to go through the process of applying just to be told no. And in the case I do get chosen, how would I pay for it? But with a month before the deadline, I decided to take a chance on myself and apply. It was a long month, but after many long nights and multiple revisions of application essays, I was finally ready to submit and wait for my fate.

Acceptance

About a couple weeks after I submitted the application, I was in the TRIO office. One of my advisors had called me over. They were reading off of an email they had just received. It was an email from COE saying I had gotten accepted to the program! All the doubt I had and the fear of letting myself and others around me down went away with that email. I was so excited about the news I froze. Everything this means to me and to the people who care for me and want to see me do great things and earn what I work hard for. It would be my first time traveling outside of the country (for context, Toronto is a 6-hour drive from my hometown), and for it to be in another continent as well was cool to me. A little later I found out my friend who had also applied had gotten in. What are the odds 2 of the 24 students selected nation-wide to participate would be both of us?! It was such a great day for myself and everyone in the office. But soon reality started to set in? How am I going to get there? What do I need to bring with me? What things do I need to do to prepare? How am I going to pay for this?

Preparation

Part of the cool thing about the COE Keith Sherin Global Leaders Program was it paid for around half of the total cost for the month-long study abroad program. The total cost was $5,000. COE paid for half, so that still left me with trying to figure out where to get $2,500 from. My way of doing it was: scholarships. With the help of my advisors I was able to find multiple scholarship opportunities, and I was awarded enough to cover the cost of the program. The only thing I had to worry about was having money to survive when I was there. As time got closer to June, when I would be leaving, more information about the logistics of traveling to Spain and the program itself came out. Luckily for me, my advisors had sent students off to the program before so they were able to help tell me what to expect. Two tasks I specifically remember having to do was send them a picture for what would be my student ID, and to get a TB test. I remember the picture being awkward because the picture was a selfie. Not like the typical professional photos you take on the first day of school. Getting and sending my TB test results were the most difficult because I had to figure out where to get it done for a cheap price. I couldn’t get it through insurance, and most of the places I found were in no way cheap, time was getting closer for me to turn it in. Luckily, I was able to find an urgent care center that did it for a somewhat reasonable price. To get to Spain I first had to get to New York because that’s where I was supposed to be meeting the rest of the cohort and the program leaders, and from New York we would be flying to Spain. My friend and I decided to travel together, it made the most sense and we could look out for each other. Fast forward to making it to New York. It was my first time in the city. It was HUGE! We got a ride to the hotel we would be spending the night in and meeting the other students. We had dinner with all of us at the hotel. It was so cool to get to meet everyone there. People from California, Texas, Puerto Rico, and other places. We bonded almost instantly. The next day we were on our way to the airport waiting to start our journey to Spain.
